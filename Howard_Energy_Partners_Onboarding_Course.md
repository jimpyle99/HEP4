## Module 1 — Company Snapshot

--{{0}}--
Let's start with the basics: who we are, where we're based, and who leads us.

Howard Energy Partners (HEP) is a privately held **midstream energy and logistics** company headquartered in **San Antonio, Texas**. The company was **founded in June 2011** by **Mike Howard**, who still serves as **Chairman and CEO** today.[^1][^2]

Howard Energy Partners describes itself as *"part of the industry that powers every other industry."* It doesn't drill for oil and gas, and it doesn't sell it to consumers — instead, it builds and operates the critical infrastructure that moves, processes, and prepares energy as it travels from the wellhead to the market.[^3]

What makes Howard Energy Partners's ownership unusual
==================================

--{{1}}--
Pay attention to this part — our ownership structure is a big reason we can think long term.

     {{1}}
Unlike most companies its size, Howard Energy Partners is backed by long-term institutional partners — the **Alberta Investment Management Corporation (AIMCo)** and **Alinda Capital Partners** — rather than traditional private equity firms. This structure is intentional: it lets the company take a **long-term view** and grow at the pace its customers need, rather than chasing short-term returns.[^4]

     {{2}}
Today, Howard Energy Partners is one of the nation's **largest private energy infrastructure companies** and a **multi-billion-dollar enterprise**, with operations across **Texas, New Mexico, Pennsylvania, Oklahoma, Kansas, and Mexico**.[^2][^5]

Meet the leader
===============

CEO **Mike Howard** holds a degree in chemical engineering from **Texas A&M University–Kingsville** and previously served as **President of Midstream** for a major Fortune 100 energy company before founding HEP. In **February 2026**, he was appointed to the **National Petroleum Council**, a federal advisory committee created in 1946 at the request of President Harry S. Truman.[^5][^6]

> 💡 **Did You Know?** The company name reflects a personal idea: founder Mike Howard wanted to build a *"meaningful endeavor"* — a values-driven company that delivers reliable energy while contributing to its communities. That phrase still shapes how Howard Energy Partners makes growth decisions today.[^3]

## Module 2 — What We Do: Understanding Midstream

--{{0}}--
The energy industry has three main parts. Let's see where we fit.

The energy industry is often split into three parts. **Howard Energy Partners lives in the midstream.**

| Segment        | What it does                                       | Example                     |
| :------------- | :------------------------------------------------- | :-------------------------- |
| **Upstream**   | Finds and produces oil & gas                       | Drilling a well             |
| **Midstream**  | Gathers, processes, transports, and stores energy  | Pipelines, plants, terminals|
| **Downstream** | Refines and sells to end users                     | Refineries, gas stations    |

Howard Energy Partners provides producers with a full suite of integrated services "from wellhead to market" — natural gas gathering, processing, treating, transportation, fractionation, terminals, and rail and marine logistics.[^3][^4]

Where Howard Energy Partners operates
===================

--{{1}}--
Our footprint is organized into regions, each serving a different need.

     {{1}}
- **South Texas** — Processes and transports natural gas from Webb County (Eagle Ford Shale) to Gulf Coast markets and Mexico, including supplying growing LNG export facilities.
- **Mexico** — The **200-mile Nueva Era Pipeline** carries gas to northern Mexico (Monterrey) via a joint venture with **Grupo Clisa**; the gas can be up to **50% less carbon-intensive** than the fuels it displaces.
- **Pennsylvania** — A large gathering system in the prolific **Marcellus Shale**.
- **West Texas** — With **Devon Energy**, large-scale gathering and cryogenic processing in the **Delaware Basin**, helping reduce flaring.
- **Port Arthur** — A first-of-its-kind terminal handling motor fuels, petrochemical feedstocks, and **lower-carbon fuels** like renewable diesel and sustainable aviation fuel (SAF).
- **Corpus Christi** — Home to the innovative **Javelina** plant, plus hydrogen sales and rail/marine logistics.
- **Oklahoma–Kansas** — The **Midship Pipeline** and the newly acquired **Superior Midstream** gathering system anchor HEP's growing Midcontinent presence.

> 💡 **Did You Know?** Renewable diesel and SAF are **100% compatible** with existing engines and infrastructure — no new equipment needed — and can cut greenhouse gas emissions by up to **80%**.[^7]

## Module 4 — Innovation & Lower-Carbon Energy

--{{0}}--
Our strategy isn't just about moving traditional fuels. Many of our facilities also address lower-carbon demand. The flagship example is the Javelina plant.

HEP's facilities are designed to **solve today's energy needs while also addressing lower-carbon demand**.[^3]

The flagship example is the **Javelina** plant in Corpus Christi. Javelina captures **waste gas** from local refineries — emissions that would otherwise be discarded — and turns them into valuable products like hydrogen, ethylene, and natural gas liquids. This is **emissions recycling**: it reduces emissions *and* creates value from waste.[^7][^8]

     {{1}}
Javelina also became the foundation for a landmark clean-energy partnership. The discarded **CO₂** from Javelina is captured and supplied to neighboring clean-fuels innovator **Infinium**, whose **Project Pathfinder** facility combines that CO₂ with green hydrogen to produce **net-zero electro-fuels (eFuels)** — a drop-in replacement for diesel used to help decarbonize heavy transport. This helped make Javelina **among the first clean hydrogen facilities along the U.S. Gulf Coast**.[^7][^9]

> 💡 **Did You Know?** In 2024, **Bill Gates** and his Breakthrough Energy team toured the Infinium eFuels facility located at HEP's Javelina Plant — describing it as a groundbreaking demonstration of clean-fuel technology. Not every day a midstream plant draws a visit from one of the world's most famous innovators![^14][^9]

Finally, the culture you're now part of. Our mission is captured in a simple phrase you'll hear often: delivering positive energy.
