## Company Overview

**Howard Energy Partners (HEP)** is one of the United States' largest private midstream energy infrastructure companies. Headquartered in **San Antonio, Texas**, HEP provides oil and gas producers with integrated midstream services from wellhead to market — including natural gas and crude oil pipelines, processing plants, liquid storage terminals, deep-water port facilities, and rail facilities.

| **Founded** | June 2011 |

| **Revenue Model** | Majority from long-term, fixed-fee contracts |

## Founder Profile: Mike Howard

Mike Howard co-founded HEP in June 2011 after serving as **President of Midstream at Energy Transfer Partners (NYSE: ETP/ETE)**, a Fortune 100 energy company, where he oversaw all midstream, transportation and storage operations. With more than 28 years of midstream energy experience, he was on the front lines of the **Shale Revolution**, helping transform energy access for the United States and its allies.

He holds a Bachelor of Science in **Chemical Engineering** from **Texas A&M University–Kingsville** and currently serves on the boards of HEP, Atlas Energy Solutions (NYSE: AESI), TXOGA, INGAA, and Jonah Energy LLC.

*Source: [HEP Mike Howard Bio](https://www.howardenergypartners.com/howard-bio/), [Atlas Energy Solutions Announcement, Feb 2024](https://www.financialcontent.com/article/bizwire-2024-2-13-atlas-energy-solutions-appoints-mike-howard-to-its-board-of-directors)*

## Timeline of Key Milestones

### 2011 — Founding

- **June 2011:** Howard Energy Partners is founded by Mike Howard in San Antonio, Texas.
- Initial investors include **GE**, **Quanta**, and **Clear Springs Energy**.
- The company begins building natural gas gathering and processing assets in **South Texas (Webb County)**, one of the state's largest natural gas producing regions.

---

### 2012–2013 — Early Growth & First Infrastructure

- **April 2012:** Company **recapitalization** — Alinda Capital Partners purchases a capital interest from GE, Quanta, and Clear Springs Energy, becoming a major investor.
- **December 2013:** The **Reveille Gas Plant** comes online (commissioned December 24, 2013), marking HEP's first major processing facility.
- **2013:** Acquisition of Webb County gathering assets from **Meritage Midstream**, adding approximately 176 miles of gathering pipelines.

*Source: [Texas Legislature Corporate Timeline Document, March 2016](https://capitol.texas.gov/tlodocs/84R/handouts/C0392016030809001/e26f3373-30ab-4d25-add6-28ecb3ed602e.PDF)*

---

### 2014–2015 — Diversification & Cross-Border Expansion

- HEP expands into **Liquids Transport & Processing**, **Terminals & Rail**, and **Northeast (Pennsylvania) Gathering**.
- **August 12, 2015:** HEP announces a **successful open season for the Nueva Era Pipeline** — a landmark 50/50 joint venture with Mexico-based **Grupo Clisa**.
  - Mexico's national electricity commission (**CFE — Comisión Federal de Electricidad**) is named as the foundation shipper.
  - The pipeline is designed to connect South Texas natural gas production directly to the industrial demand center of **Monterrey, Nuevo León, Mexico**.
  - *"This agreement with the CFE marks an important step not only for Howard Energy Partners, but for the energy and midstream industry as a whole, as cross-border relationships and business between the U.S. and Mexico continue to grow."* — Mike Howard

*Source: [HEP Nueva Era Announcement, Aug 12, 2015](https://www.howardenergypartners.com/howard-energy-partners-announces-successful-open-season-on-nueva-era-pipeline-mexicos-cfe-to-be-foundation-shipper)*

---

### 2016 — Capital Raise & Pipeline Expansion

- **May 24, 2016:** HEP completes a successful open season for the **Dos Águilas Pipeline Project**, expanding South Texas capacity.
- **August 22, 2016:** HEP announces a **$500 million private placement of preferred units**, a major capital infusion underscoring investor confidence.
- Mike Howard is named among **Ernst & Young's Entrepreneur of the Year Finalists** by the San Antonio Business Journal.
- HEP's corporate footprint at this point includes offices in **San Antonio, Houston, and Mexico City**.

*Source: [HEP News & Resources](https://www.howardenergypartners.com/news-resources)*

---

### 2017 — Port Arthur Expansion & Pipeline Milestones

- **February 23, 2017:** HEP announces plans to **significantly expand its deep-water terminal in Port Arthur, Texas** — a key Gulf Coast logistics hub.
- The **Nueva Era Pipeline** construction begins; HEP manages all construction and operations.
- HEP's asset base by this point spans:
  - 650+ miles of South Texas gathering pipelines
  - 55 miles of NGL pipelines
  - Processing capacity of 200 MMcf/d
  - Rail and terminal facilities in Three Rivers and Port Arthur, TX

---

### 2018 — Nueva Era Pipeline Enters Service

- **June 2018:** HEP completes **final tests on the Nueva Era cross-border pipeline**, marking the beginning of commercial service transporting natural gas from Webb County, Texas to the Monterrey metro area in Nuevo León, Mexico.
- The **200-mile pipeline** (later expanded to the current operational footprint) supplies gas to CFE power stations including the Huinalá and Noreste (Escobedo) combined-cycle plants.
- The pipeline reduces Mexico's industrial sector's carbon intensity by up to **50%** compared to traditional fuels.

*Source: [Global Energy Monitor — Nueva Era Pipeline](https://www.gem.wiki/Nueva_Era_Pipeline)*

---

### 2019–2021 — Continued Asset Growth

- HEP expands its **Permian Basin** operations, developing oil gathering and gas processing systems in one of the world's most productive oil basins.
- A joint venture is formed with investment partner Astatine Investment Partners (formerly Alinda Capital Partners) as **HEP Catalyst InvestCo**, focused on Permian Basin infrastructure.
- HEP deepens its presence in the **Eagle Ford Shale** (South Texas) and in **Pennsylvania** (Northeast Gathering).
- Operations in **New Mexico** are established, further diversifying the geographic footprint.

---

### 2022 — AIMCo Investment & Becoming a Major Player

- **November 2022:** **Alberta Investment Management Corporation (AIMCo)**, one of Canada's largest institutional investors, acquires an **87% ownership stake** in Howard Energy Partners, alongside existing partner Alinda Capital Partners.
  - AIMCo manages the pension and endowment funds of the Province of Alberta.
  - This transaction confirms HEP's status as a **multi-billion dollar enterprise**.
- The unique ownership structure (infrastructure-focused long-term investors rather than traditional private equity) is cited by management as enabling HEP's long-term vision and flexibility.

> 💡 **Interesting Fact:** AIMCo's 87% stake makes a Canadian pension fund the majority owner of one of the U.S.'s largest private midstream operators — an unusual cross-border infrastructure partnership.

*Source: [Caplight Company Profile](https://www.caplight.com/company/howardenergypartners)*

---

### 2023 — Record Year: $800 Million in Growth Projects

- HEP completes approximately **$800 million in growth projects** — the most expansive single-year build-out in the company's history.

**South Texas Expansion (Dos Cominos JV with NextEra Energy):**
  - Completed the **Spears Project**: a 62-mile, 36-inch pipeline and associated treating and compression facilities.
  - Completed **one month ahead of schedule** (November 2023).
  - Provides capacity for up to **1 Bcf/d of incremental natural gas** transport to Gulf Coast LNG facilities.
  - This is described as the **largest domestic pipeline project in HEP's history**.

**Permian Basin Expansion:**
  - New oil gathering and gas processing infrastructure in the Delaware Basin (New Mexico/Texas).

**Renewable Diesel Terminal (Texas Gulf Coast):**
  - New storage and logistics facilities for **renewable diesel** — HEP's first significant move into low-carbon energy logistics.

**Record Volumes:**
  - Natural gas throughput: **over 2.5 Bcf/d** (7% increase over 2022)
  - Terminalling throughput: **more than 160,000 bpd** (25% increase over 2022)

**Safety & ESG:**
  - **Zero recordable injuries** and zero preventable vehicle accidents during construction.
  - Receives **GRESB five-star ESG rating** for the second consecutive year.
  - Safety awards from leading industry associations.
  - HEP's liquidity position at year-end: **nearly $1 billion**.

*Source: [HEP Press Release, Dec 13, 2023](https://www.howardenergypartners.com/howard-energy-partners-announces-completion-of-800-million-in-growth-projects-and-record-volumes-in-2023/)*

---

### 2024 — Record M&A: Over $1.1 Billion

HEP executes a **record-breaking M&A year**, deploying over **$1.1 billion in acquisitions**:

- **Permian Basin consolidation:** Astatine Investment Partners exits HEP Catalyst InvestCo, with HEP acquiring the remaining 50% stake in the Permian Basin oil gathering and gas processing system — consolidating full ownership.
- **Oklahoma expansion:** HEP acquires gathering pipeline assets in the SCOOP and STACK plays (Oklahoma's Anadarko Basin), establishing a presence ahead of the transformative Midship deal.
- **Howard Low Carbon Solutions:** HEP creates a new division and hires **Richard Sherrill** as President of Howard Low Carbon Solutions. Sherrill brings 35+ years of energy sector experience and five years focused on **carbon sequestration** projects.
- **Scott W. Tinker, PhD** joins the HEP Board of Directors, bringing his expertise in energy access and the Switch Energy Alliance.
- **2024 Annual Report** published, highlighting HEP's core values and advocacy for global energy development.

*Sources: [ZoomInfo/Business Wire](https://www.zoominfo.com/c/howard-energy-partners-llc/346156708), [HEP Midship Announcement](https://www.howardenergypartners.com/howard-energy-partners-acquires-operating-interest-in-midship-pipeline/)*

---

### February 2025 — Midship Pipeline Acquisition

- **February 13, 2025:** HEP closes on the acquisition of **equity interests in the Midship Pipeline** from a subsidiary of **Cheniere Energy, Inc.**, one of the world's largest LNG exporters.
- **Midship Pipeline Specs:**
  - 200-mile, 36-inch, FERC-regulated natural gas transmission pipeline
  - Located in the SCOOP and STACK plays, **Anadarko Basin, Oklahoma**
  - Runs from Kingfisher County to near Bennington, Oklahoma
  - Current capacity: **1.1 Bcf/d**, expandable to **1.4 Bcf/d**
  - Connects Anadarko production to Gulf Coast and Southeast demand markets
- HEP assumes **full operatorship** of the pipeline.
- This deal closes out HEP's record **$1.1 billion+ M&A year** in 2024.

*Sources: [Business Wire, Feb 13, 2025](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20250213998958/en), [Oil & Gas Journal, Feb 14, 2025](https://www.ogj.com/general-interest/companies/article/55268030/howard-energy-partners-acquires-operating-interest-in-midship-natural-gas-pipeline)*

---

### May–June 2025 — Midcontinent Platform Consolidation

- **May 1, 2025:** HEP closes on the acquisition of **Superior Midstream**, a Tulsa-based natural gas gathering and processing company with operations across **Oklahoma, Kansas, the Texas Panhandle, and East Texas**.
  - Superior Midstream adds approximately **3,700 miles of natural gas gathering pipelines** and associated processing infrastructure.
  - This dramatically expands HEP's Midcontinent footprint.

- **May 29, 2025:** HEP increases its ownership in the **Midship Pipeline to 100%**, completing full ownership after the February 2025 partial acquisition.
  - Since assuming operatorship, HEP achieves **record throughput and utilization** across the Midship system.

> *"These transactions represent another important step in our continued commitment to disciplined growth and long-term value creation, scaling in basins that we expect to support natural gas demand for decades to come."* — Mike Howard, May/June 2025

*Source: [Business Wire, June 2, 2026](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20260602356932/en/Howard-Energy-Partners-Expands-Midcontinent-Platform-With-Strategic-Acquisitions)*

## Interesting Facts

1. **Cross-border Pioneer:** The Nueva Era Pipeline was among the first major U.S.–Mexico private natural gas infrastructure deals following Mexico's 2013 energy reform, making HEP a trailblazer in cross-border energy trade.

2. **LNG Demand Play:** HEP's South Texas expansion (the 2023 Spears Project) was built specifically in response to projected **152% growth in Gulf Coast LNG demand by 2050**, positioning HEP as a critical link between Permian/Eagle Ford production and export terminals.

3. **Long-Term Investor Structure:** HEP is financially backed by **AIMCo** (a Canadian pension fund) and **Alinda Capital Partners** — both infrastructure-focused, long-term investors rather than traditional private equity. This enables HEP to pursue decade-long development strategies rather than short-term exits.

4. **Green Rating:** HEP has received the **GRESB five-star ESG rating** for multiple consecutive years, one of the highest sustainability ratings in infrastructure investment.

5. **From Zero to $1B+ in 13 Years:** Founded in June 2011 with a small South Texas gathering system, HEP executed over **$1.1 billion in M&A in 2024 alone** — an extraordinary growth trajectory for a private company.

6. **Renewable Diesel Expansion:** While primarily a natural gas midstream operator, HEP's 2023 expansion into **renewable diesel storage and logistics** signals a strategic diversification toward lower-carbon energy products.

7. **Safety Record:** During the completion of $800 million in projects in 2023, HEP maintained a **perfect safety record** — zero recordable injuries and zero preventable vehicle accidents.
