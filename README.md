## 1\. Company Overview

### What Is Howard Energy Partners?

\--{{0}}-- Let's start with the basics — what Howard Energy Partners does and where it sits in the energy industry.

**Howard Energy Partners (HEP)** is one of the United States' largest private midstream energy infrastructure companies, headquartered in **San Antonio, Texas**.

HEP provides oil and gas producers with **integrated midstream services from wellhead to market**, including:

* Natural gas and crude oil pipelines  
* Natural gas processing plants  
* Liquid storage terminals  
* Deep-water port facilities  
* Rail facilities  
* Renewable diesel logistics

| **Headquarters** | San Antonio, Texas |
| **Founder and CEO** | Mike Howard, Chairman and CEO |
| **Operations** | Texas, New Mexico, Oklahoma, Pennsylvania, and Mexico |
| **Revenue Model** | Majority from long-term, fixed-fee contracts |
| **Ownership (2022–)** | Alberta Investment Management Corporation (AIMCo) and Alinda Capital Partners |

### What Does "Midstream" Mean?

The energy industry is typically divided into three segments:

{{0}}

**Upstream** — Exploration and production: finding oil and gas and drilling wells.

{{1}}

**Midstream** — Gathering, processing, storing, and transporting oil, gas, and liquids from the wellhead to market. This is where HEP operates.

{{2}}

**Downstream** — Refining crude oil, and distributing fuel and gas to end consumers.

## 2\. The Founder: Mike Howard

\--{{0}}-- Every company has an origin story. For HEP, it starts with Mike Howard — a chemical engineer who spent decades in the energy industry before founding his own company.

Mike Howard co-founded HEP in **June 2011** after serving as **President of Midstream at Energy Transfer Partners (NYSE: ETP/ETE)**, a Fortune 100 energy company, where he oversaw all midstream, transportation and storage operations.

Key facts about Mike Howard:

* **28+ years** of midstream energy experience at the time of founding  
* Was on the front lines of the **Shale Revolution**, helping transform U.S. energy access  
* Holds a **Bachelor of Science in Chemical Engineering** from **Texas A\&M University–Kingsville**  
* Currently serves on the boards of HEP, Atlas Energy Solutions (NYSE: AESI), TXOGA, INGAA, and Jonah Energy LLC

💡 **Interesting Fact:** Before founding HEP, Mike Howard was President of Midstream at Energy Transfer Partners — a Fortune 100 company. He left to start HEP essentially from scratch in South Texas in 2011\.

## 3\. Timeline of Key Milestones

\--{{0}}-- Now let's walk through the major events that shaped Howard Energy Partners from a small South Texas startup into a multi-billion dollar infrastructure company.

### 2011 — Founding

**June 2011:** Howard Energy Partners is founded by Mike Howard in San Antonio, Texas.

* Initial investors include **GE**, **Quanta**, and **Clear Springs Energy**  
* The company begins building natural gas gathering and processing assets in **South Texas, Webb County** — one of Texas's largest natural gas producing regions

💡 Webb County, Texas, is the state's largest producer of natural gas, making it a strategically critical location for HEP's early operations.

*Source: [Texas Legislature Corporate Timeline Document, 2016](https://capitol.texas.gov/tlodocs/84R/handouts/C0392016030809001/e26f3373-30ab-4d25-add6-28ecb3ed602e.PDF)*

---

### 2012–2013 — Early Growth and First Infrastructure

**April 2012:** Company **recapitalization** — Alinda Capital Partners purchases a capital interest from GE, Quanta, and Clear Springs Energy, becoming a major investor.

**December 24, 2013:** The **Reveille Gas Plant** comes online — HEP's first major processing facility.

**2013:** Acquisition of Webb County gathering assets from **Meritage Midstream**, adding approximately 176 miles of gathering pipelines.

---

### 2014–2015 — Diversification and Cross-Border Expansion

HEP expands into **Liquids Transport and Processing**, **Terminals and Rail**, and **Northeast Pennsylvania Gathering**.

**August 12, 2015:** HEP announces a **successful open season for the Nueva Era Pipeline** — a 50/50 joint venture with Mexico-based **Grupo Clisa**.

* Mexico's national electricity commission (**CFE — Comisión Federal de Electricidad**) is named as the foundation shipper  
* The pipeline will connect South Texas natural gas production to the industrial demand center of **Monterrey, Nuevo León, Mexico**

*"This agreement with the CFE marks an important step not only for Howard Energy Partners, but for the energy and midstream industry as a whole, as cross-border relationships and business between the U.S. and Mexico continue to grow."*

— Mike Howard, August 2015

*Source: [HEP Nueva Era Announcement](https://www.howardenergypartners.com/howard-energy-partners-announces-successful-open-season-on-nueva-era-pipeline-mexicos-cfe-to-be-foundation-shipper)*

---

### 2016 — Capital Raise and Pipeline Expansion

* **May 24, 2016:** Successful open season for the **Dos Águilas Pipeline Project**, expanding South Texas capacity  
* **August 22, 2016:** HEP announces a **$500 million private placement of preferred units** — a major capital infusion underscoring investor confidence  
* Mike Howard is named among **Ernst and Young's Entrepreneur of the Year Finalists** by the San Antonio Business Journal  
* HEP now has offices in **San Antonio, Houston, and Mexico City**

*Source: [HEP News and Resources](https://www.howardenergypartners.com/news-resources)*

---

### 2017 — Port Arthur and Nueva Era Construction

* **February 23, 2017:** HEP announces plans to **significantly expand its deep-water terminal in Port Arthur, Texas** — a key Gulf Coast logistics hub  
* **Nueva Era Pipeline** construction begins under HEP's management

HEP's asset base at this point:

* 650+ miles of South Texas gathering pipelines  
* 55 miles of NGL pipelines  
* Processing capacity of 200 MMcf/d  
* Rail and terminal facilities in Three Rivers and Port Arthur, TX

---

### 2018 — Nueva Era Pipeline Enters Service

**June 2018:** HEP completes final tests on the **Nueva Era cross-border pipeline**, beginning commercial service transporting natural gas from Webb County, Texas to the Monterrey metro area in Mexico.

* Supplies gas to CFE power stations including the Huinalá and Noreste combined-cycle plants  
* Delivers natural gas providing up to a **50% reduction in carbon intensity** compared to traditional fuels used in Mexican industry

💡 **Interesting Fact:** The Nueva Era Pipeline was among the first major private U.S.–Mexico natural gas infrastructure deals following Mexico's landmark 2013 energy reform — making HEP a pioneer in cross-border energy trade.

*Source: [Global Energy Monitor — Nueva Era Pipeline](https://www.gem.wiki/Nueva_Era_Pipeline)*

---

### 2019–2021 — Continued Asset Growth

* HEP expands **Permian Basin** operations — oil gathering and gas processing in one of the world's most productive basins  
* Joint venture formed with Astatine Investment Partners as **HEP Catalyst InvestCo**, focused on Permian Basin infrastructure  
* Deeper presence in **Eagle Ford Shale** (South Texas) and **Pennsylvania** (Northeast Gathering)  
* **New Mexico** operations established, further diversifying geographic footprint

---

### 2022 — AIMCo Investment: A Major Milestone

**November 2022:** **Alberta Investment Management Corporation (AIMCo)**, one of Canada's largest institutional investors, acquires an **87% ownership stake** in Howard Energy Partners.

* AIMCo manages pension and endowment funds for the Province of Alberta  
* This transaction confirms HEP's status as a **multi-billion dollar enterprise**  
* The unique structure — infrastructure-focused long-term investors, not traditional private equity — enables HEP's long-term vision and operational flexibility

💡 **Interesting Fact:** A Canadian pension fund is the majority owner of one of the U.S.'s largest private midstream operators — an unusual and strategic cross-border infrastructure partnership.

---

### 2023 — Record Year: $800 Million in Growth Projects

HEP completes approximately **$800 million in growth projects** — the most expansive single-year build-out in the company's history.

**South Texas Expansion — Spears Project** (Dos Cominos JV with NextEra Energy):

* 62-mile, 36-inch pipeline plus treating and compression facilities  
* Completed **one month ahead of schedule** in November 2023  
* Capacity for up to **1 Bcf/d of incremental natural gas** transport to Gulf Coast LNG facilities  
* Described as the **largest domestic pipeline project in HEP's history**

**Permian Basin Expansion:**

* New oil gathering and gas processing infrastructure in the Delaware Basin

**Renewable Diesel Terminal — Texas Gulf Coast:**

* New storage and logistics facilities for **renewable diesel** — HEP's first major move into low-carbon energy logistics

**Record Volumes:**

* Natural gas throughput: **over 2.5 Bcf/d** (7% increase over 2022\)  
* Terminalling throughput: **more than 160,000 bpd** (25% increase over 2022\)

**Safety and ESG:**

* **Zero recordable injuries** and zero preventable vehicle accidents during all construction  
* **GRESB five-star ESG rating** received for the second consecutive year  
* HEP's year-end liquidity position: **nearly $1 billion**

*Source: [HEP Press Release, December 13, 2023](https://www.howardenergypartners.com/howard-energy-partners-announces-completion-of-800-million-in-growth-projects-and-record-volumes-in-2023/)*

---

### 2024 — Record M\&A: Over $1.1 Billion

HEP executes a **record-breaking M\&A year**, deploying over **$1.1 billion in acquisitions**:

* **Permian Basin consolidation:** HEP acquires the remaining 50% stake in its Permian Basin oil gathering and gas processing system from Astatine Investment Partners — achieving full ownership  
* **Oklahoma expansion:** HEP acquires gathering pipeline assets in the SCOOP and STACK plays of the Anadarko Basin  
* **Howard Low Carbon Solutions:** New division created; **Richard Sherrill** hired as President, bringing 35+ years of energy experience and five years in carbon sequestration  
* **Scott W. Tinker, PhD** joins the HEP Board of Directors  
* **2024 Annual Report** published, advocating for global energy development

---

### February 2025 — Midship Pipeline Acquisition

**February 13, 2025:** HEP closes on the acquisition of equity interests in the **Midship Pipeline** from a subsidiary of **Cheniere Energy, Inc.** — one of the world's largest LNG exporters.

Midship Pipeline facts:

* 200-mile, 36-inch, FERC-regulated natural gas transmission pipeline  
* Located in the SCOOP and STACK plays, **Anadarko Basin, Oklahoma**  
* Runs from Kingfisher County to near Bennington, Oklahoma  
* Current capacity: **1.1 Bcf/d**, expandable to **1.4 Bcf/d**  
* Connects Anadarko production to Gulf Coast and Southeast demand markets

HEP assumes full operatorship. This deal closes out HEP's record **$1.1 billion+ M\&A year** in 2024\.

*Source: [Business Wire, February 13, 2025](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20250213998958/en)*

---

### May–June 2025 — Midcontinent Platform Consolidation

**May 1, 2025:** HEP closes on the acquisition of **Superior Midstream** — a Tulsa-based gas gathering and processing company operating in Oklahoma, Kansas, the Texas Panhandle, and East Texas.

* Adds approximately **3,700 miles of natural gas gathering pipelines** and processing infrastructure  
* Dramatically expands HEP's Midcontinent footprint

**May 29, 2025:** HEP increases its ownership in the **Midship Pipeline to 100%**.

* Since assuming operatorship, HEP achieves **record throughput and utilization** across the Midship system

*"These transactions represent another important step in our continued commitment to disciplined growth and long-term value creation, scaling in basins that we expect to support natural gas demand for decades to come."*

— Mike Howard, June 2025

*Source: [Business Wire, June 2, 2026](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20260602356932/en/Howard-Energy-Partners-Expands-Midcontinent-Platform-With-Strategic-Acquisitions)*
